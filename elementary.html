Elementary School

Grades 1 to 6

Learning Outcomes for Grades 1 & 2

  1. Arabic | Grade 1:
    • Read Arabic Alphabet
    • Read letters with diacritics (tashkeel)
    • Read three-letter words
    • Read single-digit Hindi numerals
    • Read and comprehend a list of vocabulary of daily words
  2. Islamic Studies:
    • Recognize the five pillars of Islam
    • Learn selected list of daily prayers/supplications
    • Recognize the outline of the story of creation
    • Recognize the outline of the story of Prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him.
    • Recognize that Islam is an Abrahamic religion.
    • Recognize that Judaism and Christianity are also Abrahamic religions and that all three religions originally worshipped the same God.
  3. Quran | Grade 1:
    • Memorize Chapter 101, The Calamity (Al-Qaari'a), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 100, The Chargers (Al-Adiyaat), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 99, The Earthquake (Az-Zalzala), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 98, The Evidence (Al-Bayyina), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 97, The Power, Fate (Al-Qadr), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 96, The Clot (Al-Alaq), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 95, The Fig (At-Tin), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 94, The Consolation (Ash-Sharh), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 93, The Morning Hours (Ad-Dhuhaa), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 92, The Night (Al-Lail), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 91, The Sun (Ash-Shams), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 90, The City (Al-Balad), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 89, The Dawn (Al-Fajr), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 88, The Overwhelming (Al-Ghaashiya), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 87, The Most High (Al-A'laa), by heart.
  4. Quran | Grade 2:
    • Memorize Chapter 86, The Morning Star (At-Taariq), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 85, The Constellations (Al-Burooj), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 84, The Splitting Open (Al-Inshiqaaq), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 83, Defrauding (Al-Mutaffifin), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 82, The Cleaving (Al-Infitaar), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 81, The Overthrowing (At-Takwir), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 80, He frowned (Abasa), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 79, Those who drag forth (An-Naazi'aat), by heart.
    • Memorize Chapter 78, The Announcement (An-Naba), by heart.

Please note that the above learning outcomes are for students who start at the beginning of the school year in Grade 1 and continue their studies until the end of the school year in Grade 2.

Learning Outcomes for Grades 3 & 4

  1. Arabic:
    • Identify nouns, verbs, and articles or determiners
    • Read five-letter words marked with diacritics (tashkeel)
    • Read double-digit Hindi numerals
    • Read and comprehend an extended list of vocabulary of daily words
    • Know Gregorian/Solar month names in Arabic
  2. Islamic Studies:
    • Recognize the five pillars of Islam
    • Learn selected list of daily prayers/supplications
    • Recognize the outline of the story of creation
    • Recognize the outline of the story of Prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him.
    • Recognize that Islam is an Abrahamic religion.
    • Recognize that Judaism and Christianity are also Abrahamic religions and that all three religions originally worshipped the same God.
  3. Quran:
    • Grade 3: Memorize Part 29 by heart.
    • Grade 4: Memorize Part 28 by heart.

Please note that the above learning outcomes are for students who start at the beginning of the school year in Grade 3 and continue their studies until the end of the school year in Grade 4.

Learning Outcomes for Grades 5 & 6

  1. Arabic:
    • Identify different sentence types.
    • Identify main sentence components
    • Read long words marked with diacritics.
    • Read triple-digit Hindi numerals.
    • Read and compehend a list of common Quranic words.
    • Know Islamic/Lunar month names.
  2. Islamic Studies:
    • Recognize the five pillars of Islam
    • Learn selected list of daily prayers/supplications
    • Recognize the outline of the story of creation
    • Recognize the outline of the story of Prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him.
    • Recognize that Islam is an Abrahamic religion.
    • Recognize that Judaism and Christianity are also Abrahamic religions and that all three religions originally worshipped the same God.
  3. Quran:
    • Grade 5: Memorize Part 27 by heart.
    • Grade 6: Memorize Part 26 by heart.

Please note that the above learning outcomes are for students who start at the beginning of the school year in Grade 5 and continue their studies until the end of the school year in Grade 6.

The Best of Both Worlds

Public schools do an excellent job teaching english, math, science, etc. That's paid for using your tax dollars. Why pay again for a full-day Islamic school, when you can just pay a fraction of that cost for the supplement that your child needs?

No Prior Knowledge Required

We understand that your child may be a beginner, even in High School, so we adapt our teaching accordingly.

2 Hours Twice a Week

Many parents complain that weekend school is inconvenient and is not enough. That is why our classes are two hours each, twice a week, on school days.

Character Building

Islam is not only a set of rituals, it is a complete lifestyle. Crescent Academy follows the prophetic model in building a comprehensive character.

Skilled Teachers

One has to select carefully who they take their religion from. Our teachers are skilled role models who are trained on how to deliver Islam the correct way.

Inquiry-Based Approach

Students learn better when they ask for it! Our teaching approach is to let the students ask first in order to be interested in the answer.

Convenient Location

Crescent Academy is conveniently located in the heart of Orange County close to the intersection of I-5 and CA-405 in the city of Irvine.
